# Mathematics for ML & AI

You don't need to become a mathematician. You need enough to understand why things work — and to stop getting lost when a paper mentions eigenvalues or distributions.

---

## Linear Algebra

**Essence of Linear Algebra** (3Blue1Brown)
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab
The best introduction to linear algebra ever made. Visual, intuitive, builds understanding rather than just mechanics. Watch this before touching any neural network math.

---

## Statistics & Probability

**StatQuest with Josh Starmer**
https://www.youtube.com/@statquest
Explains statistics and ML concepts from first principles with clear visuals. Start with whatever you find confusing — probability, p-values, Bayes theorem, gradient descent, PCA. Every video is standalone.

---

## ML Math specifically

**Machine Learning Mathematics** (GeeksforGeeks)
https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/machine-learning/machine-learning-mathematics/
Written reference covering the math used in ML — matrix operations, calculus basics, probability. Good for looking up specific topics rather than reading cover to cover.
