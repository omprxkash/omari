# Interview Prep

---

## System Design (do this early — don't leave it for the week before)

**ByteByteGo**
https://bytebytego.com/
Visual system design by Alex Xu. The YouTube channel is free and covers the most common system design interview questions (URL shortener, Twitter, Netflix, WhatsApp). The newsletter is also good.

**System Design Primer** (donnemartin)
https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer
The reference. Read it properly, don't just skim.

---

## Coding rounds

**LeetCode Patterns** (Sean Prashad)
https://seanprashad.com/leetcode-patterns/
Groups LeetCode problems by pattern — sliding window, two pointers, BFS, DFS, DP. Work by pattern, not by problem number.

**NeetCode — Blind 75**
https://neetcode.io/practice/practice/blind75
75 problems that cover all the patterns that appear in interviews. Start with Easy, get consistent before moving to Medium.

**AlgoMonster**
https://algo.monster/
Pattern-based approach, similar to NeetCode but with different problem selection. Good to cross-reference.

**Striver A2Z DSA Sheet**
https://takeuforward.org/dsa/strivers-a2z-sheet-learn-dsa-a-to-z
If you want deep coverage, use Striver's sheet. It goes further than Blind 75.

## Mock interviews

**Pramp**
https://www.pramp.com/
Free peer-to-peer mock interviews. You interview someone, they interview you. Real-time practice with feedback. Do this — explaining your solution out loud is a completely different skill from solving it.

---

## For research-oriented roles

**Papers We Love**
https://github.com/papers-we-love/papers-we-love
Computer science papers that are worth reading and discussing. If you're interviewing at AI labs or research teams, knowing recent papers in your domain matters.
