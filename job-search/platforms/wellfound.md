# Wellfound (formerly AngelList Talent) — How to Use It

Wellfound is the primary platform for startup job searches. It operates on a completely different model from Naukri, LinkedIn, or Indeed — companies reach out to you, not the other way around. There's no ATS layer. A real person at the company reads your profile directly.

This changes how you should think about the profile: it's not a keyword-dense document for a parser. It's a page a founder or a technical hiring manager is going to read. It needs to be interesting, accurate, and positioned for the kind of companies that post on Wellfound.

---

## Who posts on Wellfound

Mostly Series A–C startups, some pre-series companies, and a smaller number of growth-stage companies. If you're targeting:
- Product-first startups building AI tools
- Early-stage AI research companies
- VC-backed tech companies with under 200 employees

...Wellfound is the right place. If you're targeting MNCs, large product companies (Google, Microsoft, Flipkart), or government-adjacent roles, it's not the right platform.

---

## The most common Wellfound profile mistake

Setting the wrong "Desired Role" or writing a "Looking for" description that doesn't match your actual target.

If your desired role says "Frontend Engineer" but you're targeting AI Engineering, you will only receive outreach for frontend roles. The matching algorithm and the people searching are looking for exactly what you've listed.

**Desired Role:** Set this to the specific title you want. "AI Engineer", "Machine Learning Engineer", or "Software Engineer" — pick the one that appears most in roles you're applying to. Do not list "Frontend Engineer" unless that's genuinely what you want.

**Desired Tech Stack:** This field has an outsized effect on who contacts you. List the tech you actually want to work with, not everything you know. For AI Engineering:
- Python, LangChain, LangGraph, RAG, LLMs, PyTorch, AWS, Docker, FastAPI, TypeScript

Remove frontend-only frameworks from the top of this list if you're not targeting frontend roles.

---

## The "Looking for" section

This is read by founders and engineering managers who are deciding whether to reach out. It should answer:
- What kind of problems do you want to work on?
- What kind of team do you work best in?
- What stage and type of company are you interested in?

The generic version that doesn't work: *"Looking for a challenging role where I can grow and make an impact."*

A version that works: *"I want to work on AI infrastructure problems that ship to real users — RAG systems, agentic workflows, production LLM pipelines. I'm strongest in Python and TypeScript, and I want to be on a small engineering team where I can own a meaningful chunk of the stack."*

Write it in your own voice. Wellfound is read by people who care about fit, not just skill match.

---

## Salary expectations on Wellfound

Wellfound lists salary in the local currency but the interface sometimes defaults to USD — double-check that your desired salary is in INR if you're based in India and applying to Indian companies.

Set a salary range, not just a single number. Wellfound shows "₹X – ₹Y per year" in your profile, and companies filter by whether you overlap with their budget. Setting a range (e.g., ₹8L – ₹14L) is more likely to match than a single number.

---

## How companies reach out on Wellfound

When a company is interested, you get an "intro" — a short message from someone at the company through Wellfound's messaging system. You can respond, decline, or ask for more information.

What to check in an intro message:
- Who is sending it? (Founder, engineering manager, or HR?)
- What's the role?
- Is the company stage and product something you'd actually work on?

If interested, reply with your availability for a call and one or two specific questions about the role or team. This signals that you've actually thought about it rather than just saying yes to everything.

---

## Culture and values section

Wellfound has a "Wants" section where you can mark things like: "To build products", "Individual contributor track", "Challenging problems", "Team members to learn from", "Flexible remote policy".

Companies can see this and it factors into how their team matches to you. Be honest — marking "individual contributor track" when you actually want to move into management, or marking "flexible remote" when you actually want fully remote, wastes both your time and theirs.

---

## One thing Wellfound does that others don't

The profile has an "AI Interview" feature — a set of questions answered asynchronously via video or text that companies can review before reaching out. This is worth completing. A good AI interview response can turn a borderline profile match into an intro request. It's the closest Wellfound gets to letting you show personality before the first call.

---

## Keeping the profile visible

Wellfound uses "active" status — if you haven't logged in for a while, your profile is marked as inactive and companies see that. Log in at least once a week when actively searching. Updating anything (skills, desired role, availability) resets your active status.
