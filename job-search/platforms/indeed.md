# Indeed — How to Use It Properly

Indeed is the largest job aggregator globally and has a significant presence in India. It works differently from Naukri and LinkedIn: your Indeed profile is also your application — there's no separate resume screen for Easy Apply jobs. The profile itself goes through an ATS-like parser before any human sees it.

---

## Why the profile completeness warning matters

Indeed shows a message like *"Your profile can't be found by recruiters because it's missing key information"* when core sections are incomplete. This isn't just UI nudging — incomplete profiles are literally filtered out of recruiter search results.

The three sections that unlock recruiter visibility on Indeed:

**1. Skills**
Indeed says this is the #1 factor for recruiter outreach. Add skills that match your target roles exactly — use the terms that appear most often in job descriptions you're applying to. For AI/SWE:
- Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Python, NLP, RAG, LangChain, LangGraph, AWS, Docker, FastAPI, React, TypeScript, System Design

**2. Job preferences**
Set: minimum desired pay, desired job type (full-time), schedule, and location preferences. Indeed uses these to rank how relevant you are for specific openings. If these are blank, you don't get matched to anything.

**3. Work authorisation / availability**
The "Ready to work" toggle tells employers you can start immediately or within 15 days. Enable it if that's true — it moves your profile up in results for employers with urgent openings.

---

## How Indeed search works (for recruiters)

Recruiters searching on Indeed filter by:
- Job title (exact or related)
- Location
- Skills
- Years of experience
- Date "last active" on Indeed

Your **job title in the profile** matters more than you'd think — it determines which title-based searches surface you. Set it to the target role: "AI Engineer" or "Software Engineer", not a previous title like "AI/ML Intern".

Your **skills section** is the keyword layer. Skills you add here directly feed the recruiter search. Add them carefully — spell them the way they appear in job descriptions.

---

## Indeed Easy Apply vs. redirected applications

**Easy Apply on Indeed:** Your Indeed profile is sent to the employer. Your profile IS your resume for these applications — which is why completeness matters so much. The employer may also ask a few screening questions inline.

**Apply on company website (redirected):** Indeed shows the listing but clicking apply sends you to the company's own careers page. These usually feed into a proper ATS (Greenhouse, Workday, Lever, etc.). For these, submit a tailored PDF resume, not just your Indeed profile.

Rule of thumb: for companies where you don't have a referral or direct contact, and the role looks like a good fit, apply on the company website and submit a resume tailored to the specific job description — it's slower but the signal-to-noise ratio is much better.

---

## ATS considerations specific to Indeed

Indeed parses the resume you upload and uses it to auto-populate your profile. If your resume has tables, columns, or design elements, Indeed's parser will mangle the information (experience ends up in the wrong fields, dates disappear, etc.).

Before uploading to Indeed:
- Use a single-column, plain-text-friendly PDF
- No tables in the skills or experience sections
- Dates in a clear format (Month Year – Month Year)
- Standard section headers only

After uploading, check every field in your Indeed profile manually — fix anything the parser got wrong.

---

## Messages and recruiter outreach on Indeed

Indeed has an inbox where employers can message you directly. Notifications come to your email.

What to check when you receive a message:
- Is it from a named recruiter at a real company, or a templated mass message?
- Does it reference a specific role with a job description?
- Does it have a direct application link or just ask you to "apply through Indeed"?

Replying: Keep it short. Confirm interest, ask for the job description if not already attached, and ask for a call or next step. Same approach as LinkedIn InMail — short, professional, put the ball back in their court.

---

## Keeping the profile fresh

Indeed doesn't have a manual "boost" button like Naukri, but there is an equivalent: updating any section of your profile resets your "last active" timestamp and improves your ranking in search results. 

Update something small every 10–14 days while actively searching — add or reorder a skill, update your availability, tweak a preference. It takes 30 seconds and meaningfully improves visibility.
