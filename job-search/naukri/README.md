# Naukri — How it actually works

Naukri operates differently from LinkedIn. The algorithm is more aggressive about profile freshness, the recruiter tooling is different, and the way salary expectations work matters a lot earlier in the process than most people expect.

---

## How Naukri's algorithm surfaces profiles

Naukri ranks profiles in recruiter search based on a combination of:
1. **Profile completeness** — a percentage shown on your dashboard. The closer to 100%, the more consistently you show up. Fill in every section including languages, IT skills, and the "Accomplishments" fields.
2. **Freshness** — Naukri gives a visibility boost to recently edited profiles. You don't need to change anything significant — editing a skill, updating the expected salary, or tweaking a bullet is enough to trigger the boost. Do this every 10–14 days while actively searching.
3. **Keyword match** — the skills you list and the words in your summary/experience are what recruiters filter on. This is why the order of skills matters — Naukri weights skills listed earlier.

Practical tip: the biggest single improvement to profile visibility is hitting 100% completeness. Most profiles stall at 70–80% because the IT Skills section and Language section are left empty.

---

## How recruiter search works on Naukri

Recruiters on Naukri typically filter by:
- **Skills** (select from a predefined list — match the exact Naukri skill names)
- **Experience** (0–1 years, 1–3 years, etc.)
- **Location** (city or work-from-home)
- **Expected salary** (they filter out profiles above their budget)
- **Last active** (profiles updated in the last 30 days rank higher)

The salary filter is why updating your expected salary is important — if you're set at ₹6L and they're filtering for profiles at ₹8–12L, you won't appear. Set it at the realistic market rate.

## Job alerts and applying

Naukri sends job alerts by email and in-app notification when new listings match your profile. You can also set custom alerts under "Jobs" → "Job Alert".

Most useful alert settings:
- Role: "AI Engineer", "Machine Learning Engineer", "Software Engineer", "Backend Engineer"
- Location: Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Pune, Work From Home
- Freshness: New jobs (last 3 days)

**When you apply**, your resume is sent directly and the recruiter can see your full Naukri profile. Unlike LinkedIn, most Naukri applications go into a shared ATS — you don't always get a response. Following up by searching the recruiter's LinkedIn and connecting there is a reasonable next step for roles you care about.

## How recruiters reach you on Naukri

Two ways:
1. **Recruiter calls** — if your phone number is on the profile and your profile is searchable, you'll get calls from recruiters directly. Most of them are third-party staffing agencies, not in-house team members. Have your 2-minute pitch ready.
2. **Naukri messages** — recruiters send messages through the platform. You get an email notification with a preview. Reply through Naukri's inbox or use the contact details they've provided.

**Evaluating a recruiter message:**
- Does it name the company and the role? (If not, ask before agreeing to anything)
- Is the role actually relevant to your skills and target level?
- Is it a direct company recruiter or a staffing agency? (Both are fine, but they have different incentives — a staffing agency gets a fee for placing you regardless of fit)

**Replying to a relevant message:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out. I'm interested — could you share the job description 
and company name? I'd like to review the scope before we connect.

Regards,
Omprakash
```

## Salary negotiation on Naukri

Naukri profiles list an "expected salary" which recruiters can see before they even contact you. This is different from LinkedIn where salary comes up later.

Set your expected salary at the top of your realistic range, not the middle. Negotiation almost always goes down from the expected figure, rarely up. For AI Engineering and SWE roles in Bangalore/Hyderabad with this profile, ₹8–12L is realistic for a fresher/junior with strong domain depth.

If a recruiter quotes a number significantly below your expected salary, it's reasonable to ask if there's flexibility before rejecting.
