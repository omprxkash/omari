# LinkedIn Headline

The headline is the most algorithm-weighted field on the profile. It's what ranks you in recruiter searches and it's the first thing a human reads when they click on your name.

Two rules I followed when rewriting mine:
1. No past employer name — it tells the world you're currently unemployed before they've read anything.
2. Put the outcome first, then the method — recruiters search for what you do, not how you learned it.

---

## Current (old)

```
AI Engineer @ Windchill | Building Production LLM Applications | RAG & Fine-Tuning
```

Problems:
- "@ Windchill" signals this role ended — the job runs Oct 2025–May 2026, so anyone reading after May sees a past employer
- "Building Production LLM Applications" is a task, not a positioning statement
- "RAG & Fine-Tuning" is good but buried at the end

---

## Option 1 — Lead with role + specialisation (recommended for most searches)

```
AI Engineer | RAG Systems · Agentic Workflows · Graph RAG | Open to AI & SWE Roles
```

Why this works:
- "AI Engineer" matches the exact title recruiters search for
- RAG, Agentic Workflows, Graph RAG are specific enough to rank for technical searches
- "Open to AI & SWE Roles" signals availability without a frame on your photo

---

## Option 2 — Lead with research credibility (better for product companies and research teams)

```
AI Engineer | IEEE Published · Patent Filed | RAG · LangGraph · Production LLM Systems
```

Why this works:
- IEEE publication and patent are hard signals that filter out most candidates immediately
- Good for companies that care about depth over breadth
- "Production LLM Systems" distinguishes from researchers who haven't shipped anything

---

## Option 3 — Lean into the founder angle (if targeting startup-adjacent or growth-stage companies)

```
AI Engineer & Co-Founder | RAG · LangGraph · Agentic AI | Building in Public | Open to Roles
```

---

## Which one to use

Use Option 1 as the default. Switch to Option 2 if you're applying to research-heavy teams, AI labs, or companies where publications are a differentiator (think Sarvam, Krutrim, Mistral India, etc.).

Update the headline every time you start a new job search cycle — LinkedIn's algorithm gives a freshness boost to recently edited profiles.

---

## Character limit note

LinkedIn's headline limit is 220 characters. All three options above are well within that. Don't try to stuff more in — whitespace makes it scannable.
