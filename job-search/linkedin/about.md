# LinkedIn About Section

The About section is the first place where someone can hear your voice and understand what you actually care about. Most people either skip it or use it as a second resume. Neither works.

What I aimed for: a hook that names something real, a paragraph that shows outcomes not responsibilities, what I'm actually interested in right now, and a clear way to reach me. The whole thing is under 220 words so it doesn't get cut off at the "see more" fold on mobile.

## Why each part is there

**Line 1 (hook):** Directly addresses the most common complaint hiring managers have about junior AI candidates — they build demos, not production systems. Starting with this grabs the reader who's filtering for exactly that difference.

**Paragraph 1 (outcomes):** Two specific numbers — 23% and 6 hours — both tied to a named method. Anyone who reads this knows what was built and what it changed.

**Paragraph 2 (what I care about):** This is the part that makes it personal rather than generic. "Making the output explainable and measurable" is a real opinion, not a buzzword.

**The arrow list (current interests):** Three things, specific enough to be meaningful. This signals where I want to go next, which helps recruiters match me to the right roles.

**Background line:** Publications and patent are rare for someone two years out of college. Putting this near the end gives it weight without leading with credentials.

**Built in:** The tech stack in one line, no bullet soup. Recruiters scanning for Python/AWS/LangChain hit this immediately.

**Last two lines:** Location + CTA. LinkedIn has a contact section but a lot of people don't find it. Putting the email in the About makes it frictionless.
